    Stage 2: The t-test

    Choose the test from the data structure

    The correct t-test depends on whether the data come from the same participants measured twice or from two separate groups.

    t Section pairing

    Pairs with the guided learning section on paired and independent t-tests in PT research.

    Decision rule: paired means same participants measured twice; independent means different unrelated groups.

    Same people or different people?

    Toggle the design features to reveal the recommended test for the specific comparison being made.

    In this case, a paired t-test fits a within-patient pre/post question, while an independent t-test fits a separate balance-training versus usual-care group comparison. A real study with both group and time may need a fuller model, but this review focuses on the basic design cue.

    Stage 3: p-values

    How surprising is the result if H0 is true?

    A p-value is a probability under the null hypothesis. It is not the probability that H0 is true, and it is not the probability that the treatment worked.

    p Section pairing

    Pairs with the guided learning section on p-values and their role in statistical significance.

    Clinical relevance: a small p-value can still describe an effect too small to matter to patients.

    Stage 4: Confidence intervals

    Estimate plus uncertainty

    A confidence interval shows a range of plausible values for the treatment effect. The dot gives the estimate, and the bar shows uncertainty and precision.

    CI Section pairing

    Pairs with the guided learning section on confidence intervals as ranges of plausible values and indicators of precision.

    Clinical relevance: an interval can include effects that are statistically unclear or too small to matter.

    Stage 5: Sample size

    Small studies wobble; larger studies stabilize
